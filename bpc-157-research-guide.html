BPC-157: A Research Guide

What BPC-157 is, the pathways researchers study it for, and how to handle, reconstitute, and store it in the lab.

What is BPC-157?

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a synthetic pentadecapeptide — a stable 15–amino-acid sequence derived from a protein found in gastric juice. In laboratory research it is studied for its role in tissue repair, angiogenesis (the formation of new blood vessels), and cytoprotection.

Areas of research interest

  • Tissue repair & tendon research — frequently investigated in models of connective-tissue and musculoskeletal healing.
  • Angiogenesis — studied for its influence on new blood-vessel growth and the growth-factor pathways involved.
  • Gastrointestinal models — examined for gut-lining and protective pathways, consistent with its origin.
